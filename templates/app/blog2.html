A Perspective on Saving

 

Saving is paying yourself (your future self).
It's never easy to pay anyone. Whether it be, routine bills, a desirable reward, or necessary investments: it's never fun to take money out of your account.
For some reason, saving is a jaded topic. Few ask "how much you got in your savings?" and even fewer feel comfortable answering (Maybe because the answer is $0). You would think that paying yourself is the easiest thing you could ever do, yet the evidence fails to confirm this. Perhaps we never think of saving as paying ourselves.
A different perspective on saving might make things easier. After all, who doesn't like to get paid?

Why talk about this now ?

Millions of people have lost their jobs, families are stuck at home while economic issues are at an all-time high. And yes, it's time to save.

You might remember our prior blog about budgeting, where we highlighted that

your budget = money in - money out

If your budget is negative, then you've got to reduce your spending If your budget is positive, then you have some extra cash this month. (potential payday for future me!)

With this in mind, one of the most effective ways of saving is reducing your expenses. Saving is inherently tied to money in and money out. "I saved $50 today" can simply translate to, "I spent $50 less today than I intended".
Bringing things back to the pandemic, according to Economic data, spending over the past few months has been significantly reduced. Malls are closed, restaurants are closed and public gatherings have been significantly reduced. A lot of our monthly expenses come from these activities. This reduction in spending translates directly to an increase in "savable money"

Not only has our spending lowered, but there have been record contributions from the government to citizens' personal incomes. Canada and the US have recorded historical unemployment filings, and record-breaking stimulus packages are being distributed (PPE, CAREs, CERB, CESB). Looks like another avenue for saving....

So back to our understanding of a budget,

pandemic budget = more money in - less money out

  • Lower probability that your budget is negative
  • Higher probability that your budget is positive

Herein lies the "PANDEMIC OPPORTUNITY". As members of Gen-Z, we are aware that many students are back home from school with most of us in Canada receiving CESB. This has resulted in a unique summer savings situation.


How can I take advantage of this ?

We are fully aware that the world is in crisis, but this also provides an opportunity for us to come up with innovative solutions. In the words of LittleFinger, "Chaos is a Ladder". We believe this period can be used to effectively practice saving. This is an opportunity for "future you" to get a massive payday. Trust me, "future you" would be very pleased.

So how can we save ? The obvious answer is

  • Open a savings account (TFSA - Tax Free Savings Account, if you don't have one yet)
  • Put in money that you are not spending currently (savings from not eating out, groceries, going to parties, etc)
    • Truth is during the pandemic, the probability of of a positive budget is much higher. Your budget surplus. Save it!
  • Put in additional income you are receiving (CESB)
    • The first paycheck is for you .i.e. saving should become a recurring expense in our monthly budgets. A lot of our other expenses have been significantly reduced, so now we can add a new one, payments to our future self. You could honestly just call the expense "Future Self". Personally, thats how I refer to it, payments to my future self.

For additional tips on reducing your expenses or increasing your income... Head over to our Budgeting blog

In conclusion,

  • Saving is paying your future self
  • The pandemic presents a golden opportunity to save
  • Having a positive budget and having a future self expense in your budget are 2 practical ways to save.

Don't wait until it's too late! Start saving NOW !

Go ahead, create that savings account!
